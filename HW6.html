SITE Architecture

BACKGROUND

SITE or Sculpture In The Environment was an architecture firm in the 1970s known for their design of the BEST Stores, an early big-box store.

The principals of SITE came from varied backgrounds, including:

POSTMODERNISM

Postmodernism is an architectural movement that gained prominence during the 1970s. It features minimal forms with a return to ornamentation, typically used in a more abstract and over-the-top way.

BEST STORES

The Best Stores were an early big-box retail store designed by SITE. Each store was completely unique, intended to be eye-catching and distinctive. This design features part of the facade torn away and re-established in the midst of the parking lot, giving the impression that the store is moving and is a dynamic creature. Note the sliding glass doors planted in the parking lot islands.

OTHER WORKS
SITE designed many other structures, many of which were never built. One, the noteworthy "High Rise Homes," features a platform system upon which individuals can build their own designs, creating a vertical town. Ultimately, SITE seeks to create structures that overcome the constraint of being architecture, and become art that also happens to function, hence the name: Sculpture in the Environment.
